Cend, For Bain and Company.


2018, SF. Digital re-design and product video roduced for Bain & Company.

Small changes with big results.

Cend is a global E-wallet that lets you easily deposit and send money to anyone anywhere in the world.
Cend wanted to boost their profitability, and increase market share. By parterning with the Bain team, made up of consultants and designers, Cend was able to achieve much larger user-retention in onboarding and sign-up, allowing for thier customer base to grow.

As part of the ADAPT (Advanced Digital and Product Team) group, my role in the project was to re-brand the product so it could be shown to potential clients. In addition ot this digital re-design, I also led development of a 'product story' video. This included storyboarding, scriptwriting, direction, editing, and animation. The final video continues to be shown throughout the company as a success story, not only for the product, but as a new way to tell stories at Bain.

All media below is footage from the final video. All product screens and animations designed by me.




Defining the Problem

Cend's original sign-up process was lengthy, requiring nearly 5 steps and more than 18 fields.

The case team and designers looked to reduce friction during this onbaording process after studying each interaction in this process.

This exploration was later illustrated in qire-frame anmations I produced for the film.

Solutions

By protoyping, user-testing, and overall following design principles that were human-centred, ADAPT and Bain tested and iterated until a reduced and effective sign-up process was produced.

The final sign-up process was reduced down to a single page.

Video Design

Alongside storyboarding and writing the script, I edited and animated the sequences shown in the film.

Taking inspiration by famous Apple product launch films, I was careful to consider that the objective of this video was not jus toillustrate the product , as many of the previous films for Bain had been, but to tell the entire process story. By creating wireframes that grew and evolved, I illustrated iteration and change without pulling too far on charts and data.

Further beauty shots enhanced the overall immersion of the film and placed the product in the real world, or less 'protoype-y' settings.
